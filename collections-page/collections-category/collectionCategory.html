The Manchester School
The name "Manchester School" is given to the group of economists, businessmen, and lobbyists who campaigned in England in the mid-19th century for free trade, reduced government regulation of the economy (in other words for a policy of laissez-faire), and a non-interventionist foreign policy (the so-called "Little Englanders").
