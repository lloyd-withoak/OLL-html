OLL Collections


The OLL brings books and ideas together so they can converse with each other and with you. We've gathered together collections of texts from historical movements and avout big ideas that have changed the world to make it easier to allow our texts to "talk" to each other. If you're looking for curated collections focused on a specific subject, or looking for an easy way to begin to explore the library, this is a good place to start.

The Manchester School
The name "Manchester School" is given to the group of economists, businessmen, and lobbyists who campaigned in England in the mid-19th century for free trade, reduced government regulation of the economy (in other words for a policy of laissez-faire), and a non-interventionist foreign policy (the so-called "Little Englanders").

The Austrian School of Economics
The Austrian School of Economics emerged after the publication in 1871 of a trilogy of works (by Jevons, Walras, and Menger) which introduced the idea of the subjective theory value and began what has been call "the marginal revolution" in economic thought. Leading theorists in the 20th century were Ludwig von Mises, Friedrich Hayek, Murray Rothbard, nad Israel Kirzner.
